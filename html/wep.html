The Exchange Year

Worth it?

| We leave so much behind |

♦ Not perfect, but priceless ♦

The high ups and the low downs forge the character and, despite all the obstacles in the way, the tribulations are ultimately the ones contributing to the growth more than anything.

❀ The homies

My biggest fear while flying to the US: not being able to make friends.
The problem is that exchange students are alone for most of the experience. They tend to want to give everything they have to their peers, but often, on the other side, there is not the same feeling.
Luckily, I slowly found my people and everything went from 0 mph to 100 mph in seconds. I have shared a lot with these people and they have given me a perspective on the world and human behavior that I will carry with me forever.

$ The exchange students

Probably the ones who can understand exchange students best. We New Hampshire kids, as we called ourselves, sticked together for the entire experience, updating each others on our successes, and problems.
Kind people, with whom I could relate on a daily basis.

✈ Leaving Italy

I sweetly remember the day I left. Pissed off as a mental defense, and excessively focused on the task at hand, I gave my last farewell to the most important ones and quietly left.

✈ Coming home

I said my goodbyes to Francine and Bill, and with a lot of mixed feelings I reunited with my home country. Everything went back in place.
Gratefulness was the word of the day. Finally I was at peace.
