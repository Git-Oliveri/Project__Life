⇥ Track and field

After a long time off the track, I fell in love with track and field once again. Leaving school early because our races were far away in the state felt good, and fueled me with lots of energy. We sang on our yellow buses on the way to the race and did the same, regardless of the results, on the way back. Commitment was the word of the day every day.
Coach always said that showing up is 50% of the job, he was, and still is, right.

⇥ Prom

At Inter-Lakes I found out that the prom is not just another lazy, boring party organized by the school, it is a well-constructed and sophisticated celebration of all the students in the current class. Partying without alcohol and constantly observed by teachers was weird, but at the end of the night extremely fun.
Then, there was the after prom, where we put our pajamas on, chilling until 5, when we were ironically finally allowed to go home.

⇥ Graduation ceremony

The day we graduated, in typical New Hampshire way, the wind was blowing absurdly. We managed to hold our caps tightly in our hands, and get on stage receiving our diplomas, mine being ironically, but rightly so, empty.
That morning it all came to an end, and I calmly enjoyed every moment of it.
